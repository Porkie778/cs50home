CS50, officially known as "Introduction to Computer Science," has been a cornerstone of Harvard University's curriculum since 1989. Originally designed as a course for Harvard undergraduates, it began as a way to introduce students to computer science fundamentals and computational thinking, regardless of their intended major or prior programming experience.

The course underwent a significant transformation in 2007 when Professor David Malan took over as the lead instructor. Under his guidance, CS50 evolved from a traditional computer science course into an innovative and engaging learning experience. Malan introduced elements like problem sets that relate to real-world scenarios, interactive lectures, and a supportive community structure that helped make complex programming concepts more accessible to beginners.

By 2012, CS50 had become Harvard's largest course, attracting students from various disciplines across the university. Its success led to partnerships with Yale University and, eventually, the creation of CS50x, the online version available through edX. This expansion has allowed millions of students worldwide to experience Harvard's approach to computer science education, making it one of the most influential computer science courses globally.
